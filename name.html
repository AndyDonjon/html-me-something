Guitar

I have now been playing the guitar for roughly around 14 years. I first picked up a guitar back when I was 10 years old at my uncles house. I remember holding it and just being completely perplexed at how anyone could play anything on them. I sat there with this cream white electric guitar just plucking the strings aimlessly for about ten minutes with my hands until my uncle walked in on me. He asked me if I would have any interest in learning and I told him yes. He allowed me to take the guitar with me and try it out so that I could make sure I was truly interested. About a month later I had returned his guitar to him and was ordering my first guitar of my own. I would spend the next few years learning every basic fundamental my mind could wrap itself around. At the age of 16 I had formed my first band and had added two more guitars to my collection. By the age of 18 my band was playing shows regularly in my hometown and at the local venues of St. Louis. We were even lucky enough to be played on a local radio station in St. Louis. Well, here I am now, 26 years old and still playing. My collection of guitars has grown to six and I've become quite impressed with how far I've come. I'm grateful to be able to pick up a guitar and play with all of my inspirations in mind and make music that I'm passionate about.

BMX

BMX is a very interesting hobby for someone who is 24 years old to be a part of. Let me first start off by explaining what exactly BMX is. BMX, or bicycle motor cross, is an action sport that consists of riders either racing or doing freestyle riding. In my case, I do freestyle BMX. Freestyle means that I'm more of a skatepark type rider as opposed to the traditional racing style of BMX. This is a hobby that I've been a part of since I was a sophomore in high school. Me and some buddies of mine used to build dirt jumps in some woods and see how big we could get them up to. After a while I began to grow tired of the same routine of digging and maintaining the jumps every time it stormed or after the city would come destroy them so I began to venture out and into skateparks. I have traveled to about 20 different parks. With all this experience, I have gained a pretty big trick list and have become a pretty solid rider. Even with that last bit said, I'd be a liar if I said that this sport didn't come with it's struggles. This is a very dangerous activity. I have endured several injuries over the years and have landed myself in some bad situations. The worst case I ever had was when I took a fall from about ten feet in the air and came up just short of my landing which resulted in me mangling the left side of my face and breaking a few teeth out. I still love to go out and ride, but my intensity has declined slowly due to age and injuries over the years. Needless to say, I still love to show up to a park and have a great time riding while trying to help younger athletes when I can.

Sports

Sports! That one thing that millions upon millions of people in the world can come together over. Whether it's trash talking or engaging in gloating with fellow fans of sports I'm a huge fanatic. I come from a very athletic family and my Dad was one to help me from a young age. I played several sports over the years. I played baseball, basketball, soccer, golf, tennis, lacrosse, and hockey when I was lucky. I played baseball and basketball primarily throughout my schooling years. I was lucky enough to pitch a state title game twice in my baseball career. Basketball was another sport that I was able to have success in at a smaller level. In grade school I had managed to become my school's all time three point leader. At this point in my life I don't currently play any of these sports seriously anymore, but I am one to still be able to show some athleticism if I'm engaged in a pickup game. As much as I love to play sports, I enjoy to watch them just as much. I'm a huge St. Louis Cardinals and Blues fan. I watch just about every game and I have several jerseys. If I'm not busy with work or other activities on game days, I'm usually found in front of a TV cheering on my teams.
